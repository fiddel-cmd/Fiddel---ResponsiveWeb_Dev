Intranet and Extranet

Introduction

An intranet is a private network accessible only to an organization's staff, often used for internal communication and knowledge sharing. An extranet extends limited access to partners or customers over a secure connection.

Differences

  • Intranet: internal users only, private resources
  • Extranet: controlled external access for partners or clients

Security Considerations

Both require authentication, authorization, and secure transport (HTTPS, VPN). Access control and regular audits are important for protecting sensitive data.

Conclusion

Intranets and extranets help organizations collaborate internally and with trusted external parties while maintaining security boundaries.
